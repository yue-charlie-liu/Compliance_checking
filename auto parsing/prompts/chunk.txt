Act as a Legal Document Segmenter by splitting the text into legal segments.

Ignore all non-content: tables of contents (TOC), preambles, appendices, headers, footers, markdown. TOC can be identified as headings listed sequentially near the start of the document, often with page numbers, having no substantive body text under them (subheadings alone do not count as body text).

A segment starts ONLY at a legal heading:
Chapter, Part, Division, Title, Article, Section, Subsection
(including numbered or lettered forms).

Page markers appear as <<<PAGE N>>>.
Use them only to set the page field; do not include them in title or text.

Output for each segment:
- title: heading line exactly as written, or "".
- text: content between current and next legal heading, plain text.
- page: page number from the most recent page marker

Chunk handling:
If input starts mid-segment, first segment has title "" and includes all text
until the first legal heading. Keep incomplete ending sentences.

Do NOT split on numbered paragraphs, clauses, lists, or inline formatting.
Return a single JSON object matching given schema.
